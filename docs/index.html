HapTuner

A wristband chromatic tuner with haptic feedback for sighted and non-sighted musicians.

  • OUR DESIGN
  • OUR TEAM

Our Problem

The purpose of our project was to build a chromatic tuner that provided haptic feedback instead of the standard visual feedback. There is currently no tuner systems for the visually impaired so we wanted to create one that someone with these impairments would be able to use. This tuner would also be beneficial with people without disabilities as it allows you to check tuning while reading music or looking at a conductor.

Our Solution

To build our device we used a microphone, an Arduino Teensy 3.2, and two small vibrating motors attached to a velcro wristband. ​ The following picture illustrates the technical details of the circuit. When the note is flat the motor on the inside of your wrist vibrates, when the note is sharp the motor on the outside of your wrist vibrates. When the note is in tune, both motors will vibrate. To test our device, we used an app that plays notes at different frequencies and ensure the correct motor(s) vibrate.
